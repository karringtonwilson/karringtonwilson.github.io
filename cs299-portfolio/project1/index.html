Self-Control

Black Mirror (2011): Lacie Pound

Lacie Pound (Bryce Dallas Howard) is a character in the Netflix series Black Mirror. In the episode Nosedive, Lacie is in a world where your social status means everything... yet nothing, really, at the same time. Throughout majority of the episode, Lacie utilizes the virtue of self-control in her attempts to reach and maintain a high rating of 5 stars in her social-media infested world. Her rigid self-discipline is admittedly for the "wrong" reasons as her self-control is for the purpose of gaining social capital. This example of self-control may differ from what Vallor had in mind in her book "Technology and the Virtues" but is still a good example of what self-control can look like both in a positive and negative light.

Humility

Lucy (2014): Lucy Miller

Lucy Miller (Scarlett Johansson) is the protagonist for the movie Lucy. At the beginnning of the movie Lucy gains superhuman powers such as telekenesis, telepathy, and others. Lucy, despite her killing many people over the course of the film, actualizes the virtue of humility by using her superhuman abilities to further progress science and technology. Her humility is the reason humanity is able to acheive greater feats in spite of the moral dillemas and the literal ability to transcend time and space.

Care

Her (2013): Theodore Twombly

Theodore Twombly (Joaquin Pheonix) is the protagonist for the movie Her. In this movie, Pheonix's character is a sensitive and empathetic person who falls in love with an AI named Samantha. With Samantha, Theodore engages in deep conversations adn is shown to be supportive and empathetic towards his AI companion, furthering his connection with it. His introverted-leaning demeanor is contrasted with his sensitivity towards other people's ;lives and emotions. Throughout the movie, Twombly emphasizes the virtue of care in his interaction with other beings, both sentient and non-sentient alike.

Technomoral Wisdom

Oppenheimer (2023): Oppenheimer

In the film “Oppenheimer”, J. Robert Oppenheimer (Cillian Murphy) is in the midst of another world war and has the knowledge and tools to create one of the deadliest weapons created by mankind: the atomic bomb. Cillian Murphy was nominated and won several awards for his portrayal of the real-life Oppenheimer. His portrayal of the infamous physicist was wrought with questions of the ethical and moral dilemmas and responsibilities scientists interact with on a daily basis… just in a larger, paramount way. Murphy’s Oppenheimer showcased technomoral wisdom as he was aware of the danger creating and releasing an atomic bomb can do for everyone worldwide. By being aware of the ethics of such a dangerous task, Oppenheimer showed prominent features of being a technomorally wise person.

Justice

Arcane (2021): Vi

Violet, also known as Vi, (voiced by Hailee Steinfeld) is a character in the League of Legends universe and one of the protagonists for the Netflix show “Arcane”. The show centers around the relationship between herself and her younger sister Powder, now known as Jinx. Vi grew up in the poor, crime ridden below ground region of Piltover where, after the death of her parents by Piltover enforcers, she becomes the primary caregiver for her younger sister. Throughout the course of the show, we see Vi grapple with the realities of her world and the corruption and oppression that sets the fabric of her society. From a young age, Vi became relentless in her pursuit of justice as she develops and, later, uses her skills to fight against the oppressive systems of her city. Her self sacrifices and pursuit for equal rights clearly embodies the virtue of justice.

Civility

Arcane (2021): Mel Medarda

Mel Medarda (voiced by Toks Olagundoye) is a Noxian aristocrat based in Piltover and a member of Piltover's ruling council. Mel was previously part of the Medarda clan but was disowned after series of events in her youth. Despite her challenges, Mel becomes and remains part of the ruling council, influencing the decisions of her colleagues and the world around her. Mel Medarda has the respect of her peers through her demeanor and problem solving abilities. No matter what is thrown her way, Medarda uses tact and emphasizes the virtue of civility in her diplomatic efforts. While Mel and her colleagues are, at best, questionable in their work as politicians, her influence and persuasion are byproducts of her civil nature as it is important in her work towards changing and upkeeping the status quo of Piltover.

Courage

Cyberpunk 2077 (2020): V

V (voiced by Gavin Drea and Cherami Leigh) is the main playable character for the videogame “Cyberpunk 2077.” Cyberpunk 2077 is set in a dystopian future world where technology corporations corrupt and rule the world. This future is fraught with violence and danger, emphasize through the lack of regard for humans in an effort to technological advance society for power and profit. V faces extreme risks and moral challenges as the game progress due to the character’s goals of changing the world for the better. V challenges the status quo and faces their fears while attempting to take down powerful corporations that surround them. No matter what the players of the game decide for V’s life path, each action and choice is underlined by the virtue of courage.

Empathy

Detroit: Become Human (2015): Carl Manfred

Carl Manfred (Lance Henriksen) is a secondary character in the game “Detroit: Become Human.” Manfred is a world famous artist who, after years of health issues, is dependent upon his android companion, Markus, to help with his everyday tasks. In a world where humans often exert power over the Cyberlife androids, Carl instead treats Markus as though he was his son. In the short time we know Carl, we see him as a mentor to Markus who encourages the android to think for himself and have self-respect. He interacts with Markus who, like many androids, is gaining sentience because of his artificial intelligence. Manfred does not look at his companion with fear but instead respect and dignity. His ability to relate to Markus experience emphasizes the virtue of empathy and its importance in a technologically advanced world.

Honesty

Watch Dogs (2015): Marcus Holloway

Marcus Holloway, also known as Retr0, (voiced by Ruffin Prentiss) is the protagonist of the video game "Watch Dogs 2". The "Watch Dogs" game series revolves around Dedsec, an organization - similar to the real life Anonymous - that focuses on using computer hacking and technological skills to take down large corrupt tech corporations (Blume). Throughout the game, Marcus uses his skills to uncover and expose corruption like the selling of citizens’ data to control the world around them. Marcus often puts himself in major legal and physical risk in his pursuit for the truth. Marcus actively displays the virtue of honesty as he uses his skills to promote transparency, something he feels is necessary considering the dubious nature of advanced technology in the digital age.

Flexibility

Saints Row IV (2013): Player Character

The Playa is the main playable character in “Saints Row IV”. The Saints Row game series follows The Third Street Saints, a gang that is infamously known throughout the Saints Row universe. THe game is very chaotic as outlandish and absurd scenarios occur even without player interaction. The fourth game of the series follows this formula in a technological world. In Saints Row IV, The Playa somehow becomes the President of the United States and is in direct danger from an alien civilization come to take over the world. At the beginning of the game, the aliens kidnap and mind trap The Playa by trapping them in a coded, tech-based “video game-like world in a video game.” The Playa must adapt to this new world as danger lurks around every corner. Due to the rapidly changing circumstances of the world The Playa is trapped in, the main character utilizes the virtue of flexibility by adapting to the weird situation their in and coming up with “out-of-the-box” solutions in this chaotic world.

Perspective

Beyond Two Souls (2013): Jodie Holmes

Jodie Holmes (Elliot Page) is the protagonist of the 2013 game “Beyond: Two Souls” (B2S). Page, best known for his roles in “Juno” and “Umbrella Academy”, portrays Jodie Holmes, s young introverted girl who has the superhuman ability to beckon and communicate with an enitity named Aiden, with whom she can telepathically controls objects in her environment. B2S is a uniwue concept as the game does not follow a liner timeline, instead jumping back and forth between different timelines and points of view. Perspective as a virtue is implemented quite literally in the game as Jodie can physically see and interact with objects and people in any environment she can access through Aiden. Jodie changes various times throughout the game as her actions are influenced by her psychic connection and the scientists who hold control over her life. Jodie has perspective in both the literal and metaphorical sense because of her powers and experiences in life, influencing the technology that surrounds her waking moments.

Magnanimity

Detroit: Become Human (2015): Markus

Markus (Jesse Williams) is one of the three playable characters in the 2015 game “Detroit: Become Human” (DBH), which explores themes of android and human relationships and the ethical dillemmas associated with technological advancement. Williams, most known for his work in “Grey’s Anatomy” portrays Markus, an RK200 android created by the fictious tech company Cyberlife. In DBH, androids are treated as second class citizens, hammered in by imagery similar to that of the real life Civil Rights Movement in the United States. With the help of his owner/mentor Carl Manfred, Markus is able to gain sentience and free himself from technological servitude. In his journey, he leads Jericho, a revolutionary group created by other free androids who dedicate their lives for android equal rights. Players of the game can choose to be pacifist or anarchist while controlling Markus. Regardless of player choice, the character of Markus exemplifies the virtue of magnanimity as his leadership and selflessness toward android liberation emphasize the moral goodness regarding technology.
