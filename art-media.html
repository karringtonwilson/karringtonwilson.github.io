Horror Short

What the project is:

Both films you will see are part of a collection of experimental short films and video-based works created in Intermediate Video Production, a Cinema and Media Studies (CAMS) course. These projects feature themes of repetition, rephotography, and storytelling through lens-based media.

--------------------

How I came up with the idea:

This horror short was inspired by structuralist film, which is a film/video in which the presence of the camera and the making of the film become a major character in its narrative. I shot this short video using my dorm environment and neighborhood, as well as editing in sounds to give the film a creepy feeling while remaining structuralist at its core.

--------------------

Who I worked with:

Primarily individual projects, but I received feedback from my professor, Claudia Joscowicz and my peers.

--------------------

What was my role:

Director, editor, lead actor, and conceptual designer. I handled cinematography, post-production, and narrative structuring.

--------------------

What I learned:

How to convey meaning through visual storytelling, the impact of editing techniques on perception, and how to refine my artistic voice through iteration.

--------------------

What challenged me:

Balancing conceptual depth with accessibility for viewers. Some ideas felt too abstract, so I worked on making them more engaging without losing artistic intent. I also want to edit these further in the future to make the narrative tighter and possibly expand on them further.

--------------------

How it relates to my major:

It bridges my Media Arts and Sciences background, integrating film theory with interactive and immersive storytelling techniques.

Black Film Archive Rhythm Game







(Work in Progress) A rhythm game, made for ARTS 255, that celebrates Black film history. This is based on the Black Film Archive

What the project is:

A rhythm-based game that celebrates Black cinema and music, incorporating visuals and audio from iconic Black films found in the Black Film Archive. Players match beats while the related music video plays in the background. After some gameplay, players have to correctly answer which black film or show the song featured in, is referenced to, or is connected to.

--------------------

How I came up with the idea:

I wanted to merge my love for film, music, and interactive design into a game that both entertains and educates about Black cinematic history. I wanted to emphasize the connection between music and storytelling. This was inspired by games like Dance Dance Revolution, Friday Night Funkin, and Beat Saber but with a black, cultural focus.

--------------------

Who I worked with:

I developed this game by myself with some help from reddit forums on proper BPM implementation. As this is an HTML5 game, I focused on UI/UX design and gameplay mechanics.

--------------------

What was my role:

I created the design, the idea, and chose what movies, shows, and songs to include. Currently, I am debugging rhythm mapping to align visuals and music better.

--------------------

What I learned:

How to design an interactive rhythm game, the importance of rhythm mapping in beat game mechanics, and what songs have been featured in some of the media I found.

--------------------

What challenged me:

Fair use considerations when incorporating film and music clips, ease of play, and timing with BPM. Also, enhancing interest through further game mechanics (adding lives, adding quizzes, etc.).

--------------------

How it relates to my major:

It combines my background in Media Arts and Sciences, bridging game design, cultural studies, and interactive storytelling.
